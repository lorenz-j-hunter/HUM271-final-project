### Impetus
*"The issue of public acceptance of masculinity is important to men who are left behind in society because the anger directed at women and progressivism from them damages others as well as themselves. To be specific, we ask: To what degree do perceptions of toxic masculinity contribute to political polarization in the U.S.?"*

This quote seems to be very ignorant of women's struggle for equity and equality in the modern day, and it is.
From a feminist's point of view, there is no anger directed at men from progressives and women in general.
Men continue to be at the forefront of some of the world's worst atrocities, while women have strong tendencies to
manifest all of the good virtues that we hold in society. They are also better educated than men and more employed
than them.

However, we question this point of view anyway. We assert that women's perceptions of masculinity and the way
they conceptualize certain cohorts of men do represent, or at least seem like, anger to the target populations.
It follows that these would women's image to men in society, affecting culture.
However, it would be immoral to simply believe this without offering some justification. That is why we conduct
a study of public opinion to see if this assertion is true.

As a last word, the political polarization seen in recent years seems to have coincided with women's outperformance
in a number of metrics. The macho ethnonationalism seen in Republican rallies appeals to men only.
In society, we sanctify democracy and generally assume there is nothing wrong with it in principle.
But can it solve a crisis like this? With the results of this study, we hope to reaffirm the importance of 
standing up to women's rights while also constructively criticizing their rise to power.
We believe there is a third way not just for the political arena, but for relations between men and women.

### Goal
The app aims to get data on women's perception of masculinity within society.

With this site, a user will be able to (1) select a website to scrape and (2) create a CSV which contains
columns. One column (nodes) will be the authors of posts. The second column (gender) will be the gender of
said author. The third column (edges) will be the feed they are in, or the recipient of the message. The third
column (edge width) will be the overall expressed disdain of masculinity in particular within the text.
